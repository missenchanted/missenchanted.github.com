Olay Regenerist Regenerating Serum – Review

Hello girls! I have never had perfect skin, suffering constant from breakouts, blemishes etc. Years of clean ups at parlours and over enthusiastic  use of scrubs have also left open pores around my nose and cheeks. So I am always in search of pore minimizing or refining creams whenever I travel overseas. I picked the Olay Regenerist Regenerating Serum  when I was in Malaysia last year at Watsons (a store like Health and Glow + pharmacy) in Malaysia for 49 Malaysian Ringgit which works out to roughly Rs 850. Price: Rs 1299 for 50 ml At first I tried using the serum on a freshly cleansed face under the Olay Regenerating day cream but I somehow found using both together quite heavy and my oily skin felt a bit greasier. So I used only the day cream first till it got over and kept the serum aside for a while. I recently started using the serum by itself. The texture is light and gets absorbed easily but I do not find it as light as a serum should be. I cannot immediately layer another product over it. So I use it immediately after using face wash and let it sink in for an hour or two. I then apply sunscreen or my day cream a while later. I find that applying it this way works better with this serum. I have been using the Olay Regenerating Serum for over six weeks now but do not find any visible difference in my skin or improvement in skin texture. The only benefit is that it is a bit moisturising which any moisturiser or moisturising sunscreen can provide. I did not break out, so no damage done! Pros
  • Quite Moisturing
  • Gets absorbed easily
  • No breakouts
  • Pump dispenser so hygienic to use
Cons
  • Texture is on the thicker side for a serum
  • Did not see any effect on my pores
  • Pricey
I doubt I will repurchase this. I think the use of a regular light moisturiser under sunscreen or foundation would suffice. I am convinced now that nothing really works other than a good make-up primer occasionally.

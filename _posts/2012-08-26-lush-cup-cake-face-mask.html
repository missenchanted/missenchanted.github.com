Chocolate is my weakness, specially when I dont feel good.. no one can keep me away from them. I need to pick up anything thats chocolate based and this one was no exception.. I picked this one up just for my lust for chocolate...

Price: Rs.480 for 100gm

Claims: Chocolate heaven for oily and teenage skin. Its action removes excess oil and calms breakouts. Rhassoul mud cleans and soothes, linseed, cocoa butter and cocoa powder soften the skin. Balancing peppermint, spearmint and sandalwood oils complete this effective and delicious experience. Apply to clean skin, avoiding the eye area. Leave for five to ten minutes, then rinse away with warm water. Keep chilled.

Contains:

My Experience: The name indeed is very yummy, but when compared to its counter parts I did not find this a HIT on my list. Its definitely a fresh mask, which looks and smells yummy. Controls oil to an extent and gives a smooth feel. But I usually get these effects from any normal clay based mask. The only difference you find over regular clay is that it doesn't dry up and gives that stretchy feeling.

Although I enjoyed this mask, but shelling out big bucks I might not recommend, you can opt for either Brazed Honey Mask or Love Lettuce

I use lush cleansers as masks also... nice na..... but cant help as they stay fresh for 3 months.

I will surely recommend these to gals who are looking out for natural stuff in skin care and something that smells yummy. This mask definitely does what it claims, but nothing exceptional.

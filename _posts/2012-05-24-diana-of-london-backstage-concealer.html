I am gifted with good skin, thanks to Mom... love you. But then those occasional break out leaving behind marks and eye tugging and pulling giving those Dark circles that a good concealer becomes the need of the hour.

Price: Rs. 575 got 5gm Diana of London says:

FOR IMPECCABLE & PRECISE MAKE-UP

  • Extreme coverage, comfortable & easy to use.
  • Corrects blemishes, long lasting.
  • Skin is nourished & protected.
  • Perfect for touch – ups. It has a delicate powder finish, does not require face powder for fixing it.
  • Vitamin E, Olive oil, Bisabolol, Aloe-butter’s soothing properties, perfect for contour of eyes & eye lid.
Contains: Available shades:              I got Nude Beige  

My Experience: The highlight of the concealer has to be the packaging, it is just so convenient and handy. The formula is creamy and feels light. Gives a good coverage for those dark circles and veins without a whitish cast. Although it gives a powder finish and needs no setting, but I still like to top it with some powder to make it uniform with the foundation.

I first use my tinted moisturizer or Maybelline BB cream, then dab the concealer over problem area. A slight touch with finger or a sponge will set it, and then top it with a translucent powder. Now days I am using the Diana of London powder and its just beautiful, will review that soon.

What I like:
  1. Like the packaging, its quite sturdy, handy and convenient,
  2. Lots of shades to choose from,
  3. Its creamy and glides and just needs few dabs and sets properly,
  4. Covers problem areas, good coverage,
  5. Easily blendable and does not settle in fine lines,
  6. Good stay power,
What I did not like:
  1. I find it a bit pricey, but there are some great discounts online,
  2. I think the Brand needs to work on their packaging, such as colors and make it more attractive.

Apart from the boring packaging I did not find anything wrong with product. If you happen to get a match for your skin tone, do pick it up and you won't regret.

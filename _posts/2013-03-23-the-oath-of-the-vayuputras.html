The Oath of The Vayuputras Review

Om Namah Shiva...

Har Har Mahadev !!!!!!

After a very long wait, The Oath of The Vayuputras was released in Feburary end. Shiva triology is undoubtedly a commendable piece of work from an Indian writer after a very long time. The First two books in the series, The Immortals of Meluaha and The Secret of The NagasÂ were so well scripted and narrated that I fell in love with the character Shiva. I wont say that the final part is a perfect one, it has its own loopholes(in my view) but still the story is well presented. What disappointed me a little was the end of the Trilogy and the long narrative war stories that turned my read a little boring. Don't worry I am not going to spoil an excellent read for all of you, but I do have some serious questions in my mind and if by chance you have the answer to those do drop a comment. I am not able to get the answer to the following questions,
  1. Book mentions the rein of Ram that was before Krishna(another Vishnu avatar who is never mentioned in the story). A refrence is made to Bhagwad Gita as the Book of Songs. Does that mean Bhagwad Gita exsisted before Krishna ?
  2. Lady Sati's apparition giving different advice to both her son, did sound a bit confusing. Did she really want to avenge or forgive ?
Since this book is a piece of fiction based on mythology, and most of the events in the past two books did relate somehow to my knowledge of Hindu religion. But the following again left me puzzled,
  1. The use of mass destruction weapon could not be related to mythology... Can it be ?
The final two chapters were on a fast track. I had to stop and think why exactly Brigu changed his mind over someone's death. But then I was convinced with my own answer. If you have read the first two books, this book is a must must read. If you have not read any of the book, then all three books are a must must read. Going by Amish's word I guess next Trilogy is based on Mahabharat... Do you also think so ?

Revlon has made a late grand entry in India while it is already being loved and raved about amongst beauty & make-up enthusiasts. Lip butters sound way too exotic to my ears somehow.

Price : 600 INR

Revlon says: - Hydrating Mango, Shea and Coconut butter formula boosts lip moisture by 156% - Pampering gel formula provides super charged hydration and glides on lips buttery smooth - Sheer to medium colour with buttery shine - Available in 20 luscious shades

Only a few selected shades are available in India. Availability is an issue, I wanted Sweet Tart but it was not available at the counters but I could find it online.

My Experience with Revlon Color Burst Lip Butter: The packaging is indeed colorful and chic, the color of the above case is an insight of the color of the stick. The texture is buttery, hydrating and moisturizing enough to sustain the Indian winter dryness. But I somehow find the staying power too low than expected, first time It wore off in 3 hours and in 3.5 hours the next. The second time, I wore a chapstick underneath to fill the chaps, if any. It leaves slight tint behind.

The pigmentation is far better than a tinted lipbalm but not good as that of lipsticks. Some colors may not appear on pigmented lips. Revlon Colorburst Sweet Tart is a soft cool toned pink, almost like Barbie pink. Sweet tart is moderately tinted and does show up on my pigmented lips, though I double swipe.

What I liked about Revlon Colorburst Lip Butter:

  1. The packaging indeed is chic and attractive. Cap is also same color as the stick, so it makes it easy to identify.
  2. Texture is extremely buttery and moisturizing.
  3. The glossy finish, just like that of a Lip gloss. Except the stickiness.
  4. Pigmentation is way better than a tinted lip balm
  5. It has a mild Vanilla fragrance to it, very very mild. But Revlon claims it to be fragrance-free.
  6. It leaves a tint behind, which somehow looks natural and I like it.

What I disliked:

  1. The price! It is expensive for 600 bucks.
  2. I am somehow skeptic about the pigmentation, it may not show up on pigmented lips which most of Indian women have.
  3. The stick is too soft, it might just melt in the sun.
  4. Availability is an issue, I could not find the shades I want at the counters at Westside and Central.
  5. This may not go with chapped lips, the color might just accentuate the chapped corners.

Verdict: I tend to reach out for this, almost all the time. It has somehow replaced my regular chapstick and balm. I personally think every girl should own this cute tube, but only after learning about the shades. Revlon Color burst works like a lip butter, gloss and lipstick at once, which makes it a multi-tasker.

Will I purchase Revlon Color Burst Lip Butter again? Yes, this time after trying out Pink Truffle at the counter.

Rating: 4/5

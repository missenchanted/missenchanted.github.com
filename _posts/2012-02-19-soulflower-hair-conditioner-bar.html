I recently picked this solid conditioner as I wanted to try out something natural for my hair time, no more SLS and parabens. But this turned out to be a nightmare.. hmmm even more than that...

Price: Rs.400.00

SoulFlower says: Soft, manageable hair.Keeping your hair soft, shiny & strong is tough.This unique herbal formula combines the old with the new, brings out the hair's natural color overtones & brilliance, improving shine & volume. Natural herbs work on premature greying & sleeplessness due to a stressful lifestyle. Notice the difference to the quality of your hair as well as the health of your scalp in one use! Wash with Soulflower shampoo bar & rinse thoroughly.

How to use: Apply a small amount of conditioner to the hair and scalp & gently massage in.Leave on for 10-15 minutes, then rinse thoroughly & dry the hair. Perfect for all types of hair.

Contains : Essential oils of jasmine, clary sage, tea tree & ylang ylang, jojoba & almond oils,cocoa butter,shea butter, bhringraj, amla, bhrami, hibiscus flowers, methi seeds, aloevera & flaxseed gel, glycerin, aqua, olive, castor, flaxseed, sesame oil.

My Take on the product : First time I used the product as mentioned on the bar, used shampoo to cleanse and followed by this bar and left it for 7-8 min. to my horror after wash my hair felt like dry hay, I could not imagine what happened did I use it too much or did I use it the wrong way. I gave it a try again using minimal quantity but still same. Ok so third time I directly used this on my oiled hair followed by my usual conditioner. My hair felt squeaky clean and oil was removed oh yeah but they still had a weird texture.

I would also like to add here that my hair don't react well to home made reetha and shikakai also and this had a similiar effect on me. Now the question is how can a conditioner remove oil from my scalp, is it really a conditioner or this is a shampoo with a wrong name.

Can anyone please tell me if I am using this the wrong way, else I am gonna use it as a shampoo for ultimately oiled hair :)

I am not going to purchase this again, Condioner is such a misnomer word for this product, its reetha and shikakai or a shampoo :( [important]Edit: Soulflower team has forwarded the review to their formulation team.[/important]

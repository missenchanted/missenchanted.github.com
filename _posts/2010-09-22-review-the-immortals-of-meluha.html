Book Review: The Immortals of Meluha

This book has created a hype amongst the Indian readers and it deserves all applauds. Amish has skillfully woven his imagination using the skeletal history with no records and some religious myths. Once you have started, I bet you cant keep it down, Amish has something to deliver with every page. He based his imagination with what we all believe in Shiva the Legend,The God of Gods. His story from a tribal leader to his holiness. The code of conduct in Ram Rajya, the lost river of Saraswati, Lady Sati and disciples Nandi and Bhadra, and above all the mystery of Somras and Nagas. Its such beautifully crafted and narrated.......The story is an excellent piece of fiction, its not meant to be a study of history or religion. I related the book to the Trilogy "Lord of the rings", but with Indian characters and Indian learning.Something that we can percieve more easily :) I just loved the book and cant wait for the Part II..... Way to Amish!!!! Cheers

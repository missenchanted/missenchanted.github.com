Looks of a compact and additional responsibilities of wet intense coverage. Too much expectations stand in front of the Wet & Dry Pancake from Diana of London. So did this pass my review test... read on :)

Price: Rs. 895

About: This lasting foundation empowers your face with a soft and velvet veil. The flawless effect grants a uniform skin tone, minimizes small imperfections and gives a matte effect to your face, providing a natural brilliance. In addition, its two ways of application, perfectly adapts the level of coverage: with wet sponge, for a more intense release of color; with dry sponge for a more natural look    Shades Available:    

My Experience: Being gifted with oily skin, a good compact is a must for me. Before this I have actually never used wet sponge with compact, but I had to give it a try. As a compact its not as finely milled as its counterpart Diana of London Absolute Stay Compact face powder but does quite decent work. When used dry its gives me matte look for 4-5 hours, and when used wet it gives a dewy look. I noticed that when used wet, it does not give much coverage but only a natural dewy look.This has a beautiful pattern, but not as beautiful as the DOL Absolute stay,

  [caption id="attachment_3844" align="alignleft" width="900"] Used with wet sponge here[/caption] What I like:
  1. Comes with a decent size mirror and good quality sponge,
  2. Gives coverage and a matte look when used dry,
  3. Gives a dewy look when used wet,
  4. Matte look lasts me 4-5 hours,
  5. When used wet, not much coverage but natural glow (Can be a con for others),
  6. Its not chalky, unlike many similar compacts in the market.
What I did not like:
  1. Bit pricey, but check out discounts online,
  2. They can improve a lot on packaging, almost everything is off white and very symmetrical & boxy :(
I might not pick this up again, but will definitely recommend this to who don't want to carry 2 products both for matte and dewy look. Have you tried Diana of London? You can catch them @ Website, Facebook, & Twitter *Product was sent by the company, but that has not affected the review.
